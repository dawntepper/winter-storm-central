Welcome to the StormTracking blog. This is where the long-form companion content lives alongside the live radar and alert tools — context, prep guides, and seasonal outlooks that don't fit into a real-time map.

## What you'll find here

A mix of practical and exploratory posts:

- **Prep guides** — what to actually do before, during, and after specific kinds of severe weather
- **Seasonal outlooks** — what the data is saying about hurricane season, winter storms, fire weather, and more
- **Context for active events** — when a major storm is unfolding, the blog is where the "why" and "what's next" lives
- **Behind-the-scenes notes** — how the radar and alert systems work, what data they pull from, and what they don't tell you

## Why a blog?

The radar and alerts pages are great at answering *"what's happening right now?"* — but they can't really answer *"what should I be doing about it?"* or *"is this normal for this time of year?"*. Those questions deserve more than a notification.

If you want updates when new posts go up, subscribing to weather alerts at the site footer also opts you into occasional blog roundups (no spam — only when there's something worth reading). Or grab the [RSS feed](/feed.xml).

Stay safe out there.
